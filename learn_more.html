History of Saipem

Saipem (Società Anonima Italiana Perforazioni e Montaggi) was established in 1957 as part of the ENI Group in Italy. The company was created to provide engineering, drilling, and construction services to support the rapid expansion of the oil and gas industry during the post-war industrial growth period.

During the 1960s and 1970s, Saipem expanded its operations internationally, particularly in Africa, the Middle East, and South America. The company became recognized for its expertise in pipeline installation, offshore platform construction, and large-scale industrial plant development.

In the 1980s and 1990s, Saipem invested heavily in offshore technology and specialized construction vessels. This allowed the company to execute complex deepwater projects, subsea installations, and challenging marine operations in harsh environmental conditions.

Entering the 2000s, Saipem strengthened its global engineering and procurement capabilities by delivering large EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction) contracts. These included LNG liquefaction plants, refineries, petrochemical facilities, and offshore floating production units (FPSO).

Today, Saipem operates in more than 60 countries and employs thousands of professionals worldwide. The company has evolved from a traditional oil and gas contractor into a key player in the global energy transition, focusing on innovation, sustainability, and advanced engineering solutions.

What Does Saipem Do?

1. Offshore Engineering & Construction

Saipem designs and builds offshore platforms, subsea systems (SURF – Subsea, Umbilicals, Risers, and Flowlines), and floating production facilities. The company operates a fleet of advanced construction vessels capable of deepwater pipeline installation, heavy lifting, and subsea infrastructure development.

2. Onshore Engineering & Construction

The company executes large-scale onshore projects including LNG plants, refineries, gas treatment facilities, petrochemical complexes, and industrial plants. Saipem provides complete EPC solutions, managing projects from initial design to final commissioning.

3. Offshore Drilling Services

Saipem owns and operates a fleet of drillships, jack-up rigs, and semi-submersible platforms. These units are used for exploration and production drilling in shallow waters and ultra-deepwater environments.

4. Onshore Drilling Services

The company also provides land drilling services using high-horsepower rigs suitable for operations in deserts, jungles, and remote regions. Specialized services include geothermal drilling and unconventional gas exploration.

5. Energy Transition & Sustainability Projects

In response to global climate challenges, Saipem is actively involved in renewable energy and decarbonization projects. These include offshore wind farms, hydrogen production facilities, carbon capture and storage (CCS), and environmentally sustainable engineering solutions.
